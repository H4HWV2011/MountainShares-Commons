## What you're agreeing to

By enrolling in, contributing to, or using MountainShares, you accept the Terms and agree
to follow the Program Rules, phase specifications, safety policies, and duly adopted
governance decisions. The Terms apply to everyone who uses the Commons — residents,
volunteers, organizers, vendors, institutional partners, stewards, and administrators.

## The nature of the system

MountainShares is a **community mutual-credit and prepaid-value system** for local
participation, exchange, recognition of contribution, and community coordination. It is
**not**:

- a bank account
- a deposit account
- a savings product
- an equity program
- a promise of profit

**EMS and M$ are community instruments used inside the Commons.** They are not shares,
stock, partnership units, or ownership interests in Harmony for Hope, Inc., the DUNA, KTS,
or any other entity. Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or
profit-participation rights. No participant should treat EMS or M$ as a speculative asset
or financial investment.

The buying-power expansion schedule (described in [`token-model.md`](token-model.md)) is
**conditional, reserve-gated, revocable, and not vested.** No participant holds a vested or
guaranteed right to any future rate before a reserve threshold is cleared.

## Money services

Payment loading, fund custody, and cash-out are operated by the **MountainShares DAO/DUNA
as the money-services licensee of record** (WV Money Transmitter License, FinCEN MSB
registration, surety bond, BSA/AML program). **H4H is not the money transmitter.** Direct
money-services questions to the DUNA compliance function.

## Securities, tax, and benefits — under review

The securities-law treatment of EMS, M$, and the phase-based conversion mechanism is
**under review by securities counsel, and the program makes no representation as to whether
any of them is or is not a security.** Benefits and tax consequences of participation are
**under benefits-counsel review; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling has been
obtained.** You are responsible for obtaining independent legal, tax, and benefits advice
specific to your circumstances. The program does not provide individualized advice.

## Benefits-sensitive protection

Participants whose public benefits could be affected by participation are protected by
**Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA)** safeguards. For these accounts, M$ cash-out is
**disabled by default**, with exceptions documented case by case; benefits status is
handled with privacy protection.

## Your responsibilities

- Use the system honestly and within the Program Rules and safety policies.
- Provide accurate information where verification is required.
- Seek your own professional advice on legal, tax, and benefits questions.
- Understand that recognition (EMS) is not a guaranteed monetary return, and that
  buying-power rates are conditional and can revert.
