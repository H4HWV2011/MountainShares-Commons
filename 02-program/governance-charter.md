# Governance Charter

MountainShares is governed as a community commons, not an equity vehicle. This page
summarizes how decisions are made, who holds what authority, and — importantly — what no
vote can override. The governing document is the *MountainShares DAO Governance Charter*
(currently a draft for board and community review prior to Phase 0); this is a faithful
summary, not the charter itself.

## The governing principle

Authority over the commons is exercised **for community benefit**, with transparency,
fiduciary discipline, and explicit safeguards against private inurement, hidden
extraction, or implied ownership over the commons, treasury, or resident economy.
MountainShares is designed as a community system, not an investor-return vehicle.
Participation creates **no equity or ownership** in H4H, the DUNA, or KTS.

## Who holds what

The three-box structure (detailed in [`../06-legal/entity-structure.md`](../06-legal/entity-structure.md))
maps onto governance as follows:

- **MountainShares Commons / DUNA** — the community-owned governance and mutual-credit
  system: the EMS recognition ledger, the M$ closed-loop system, proposal and voting
  processes, treasury and reserve oversight, and benefits-safety rules. It is the
  money-services licensee of record. It is **not** a corporation organized for investor
  return or token appreciation.
- **Harmony for Hope, Inc.** — initial charitable anchor and operational sponsor during
  the transition period. H4H does **not** own participant EMS balances, resident M$
  balances, or community governance power as private property, and does not issue founder
  equity through any resident-facing account structure.
- **Kidd's Technical Services** — the founder-side technical and commercial vehicle for
  the ALLIS platform IP, entirely separate from the commons. Any founder capital,
  licensing revenue, or private appreciation mechanism runs through KTS-side instruments —
  never through EMS, the DUNA, or H4H-administered accounts.

## The founder is a steward, not an owner

The founder's role in the commons is **steward**. The founder may hold leadership, design,
technical, or governance roles, but those roles create **no ownership** of the commons, the
DUNA, the treasury, resident EMS balances, participant M$ balances, or any community
reserve. Founder authority exists only to the extent granted by the charter, the program
rules, and duly adopted governance procedures.

## How voting works

Governance operates on a **one verified identity (UEI) = one vote** principle, once minimum
participation criteria are met. Decisions escalate by impact:

- **Ordinary decisions** — simple majority, within safety limits.
- **Structural or high-impact changes** — **66% super-majority**, plus board ratification
  and advance notice where a protected floor is involved.
- **Phase 3 surplus decisions** (surplus retention, phase initiation, distribution-interval
  changes) — **75% super-super-majority**.

The DAO governs genuinely adjustable parameters: EMS→M$ base rates within safety bands,
per-user caps, promotional pool sizes, phase-transition advisory votes, and certain
thresholds.

## What no vote can override

This is the heart of the safety design. The DAO **cannot** override:

- **Hard safety triggers** and the automatic reversion cascade (if reserves fall below a
  threshold, the higher rates switch off automatically — no vote can keep them on).
- **Reserve-ratio safety bands.**
- **Legal and compliance requirements**, including the money-services obligations held by
  the DUNA and the nonprofit obligations held by H4H.
- The **protected allocation floors** (Treasury Reserve and H4H Operations) without the
  full super-majority + board + notice process.

Governance is participatory *within* a frame of non-negotiable safety and legal
constraints. The community can steer the system; it cannot vote the guardrails away.

## Transparency

Material governance rules, treasury conditions, reserve ratios, and structural roles are
disclosed in a clear, auditable manner. Reserve ratios are calculated daily and published;
governance actions are recorded. The intent is that the separation of the three boxes and
the operation of the safety bands are demonstrable in the running system and its audit
trail, not merely asserted on paper.
