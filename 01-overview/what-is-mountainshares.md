# What is MountainShares?

MountainShares is a community economic system built for West Virginia and the
wider Appalachian commons. It is sponsored and operated by **Harmony for Hope, Inc.**
(H4H), a 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Mount Hope, WV, as a charitable,
community-benefit program — currently in its West Virginia pilot.

In one sentence: MountainShares recognizes the community labor the formal market
overlooks, lets part of that recognition become spendable local buying power, and
keeps that value circulating inside the region instead of leaking out of it.

## The problem it answers

Appalachia's extraction economies are leaving, and what's replacing them often
extracts in newer ways — absentee and out-of-state ownership, platform monopolies
that capture local commerce, and black-box systems that make consequential decisions
with no community accountability. MountainShares is a deliberate counter-design: an
economic layer rooted in a specific place, owned and governed by the people who use
it, and built to be inspected and challenged rather than trusted blindly.

## What it actually is

A **closed-loop** community economic system with several parts working together:

- a **recognition ledger** that records verified community contribution
- a **spendable community-currency layer** for in-network purchases and commerce
- a **reserve-backed treasury** designed to survive shocks, fraud, and regulatory costs
- a **merchant and community commerce network** that recirculates value locally
- **free public internet infrastructure** (Wi-Fi and LoRa community mesh), never paywalled
- a governed path toward eventually returning surplus to long-term participants

It is **not** a speculative token project. It is a governed mutual-credit and
prepaid-value commons with hard safety triggers, legal boundaries, and an explicit
anti-extraction design.

## The two value layers

| Layer | What it is | How it behaves |
|---|---|---|
| **PMS / M$** (Purchased MountainShares) | Value a participant loads with USD through an approved payment channel | Immediately spendable in-network, subject to fees, caps, reserve checks, and compliance controls. Loading "$100" results in a 100 M$ balance; fees are shown on top, never silently skimmed. |
| **EMS** (Earned MountainShares) | Recognition granted for documented community contribution — caregiving, mutual aid, education, cultural preservation, mapping, governance, connectivity stewardship | Begins as recognition in an unlimited ledger; only part converts into spendable M$, under strict caps and reserve-linked rules. It is the lasting record of who contributed and how. |

## How it stays safe

Two reserve metrics, continuously monitored, govern the system: an **Operational
Reserve Ratio** for day-to-day spending safety, and a **Treasury Reserve Solvency
Ratio** for the long-term health of the recognition-to-buying-power design.
MountainShares scales through three governed phases — Launch & Safety, Regional
Growth, and Surplus Distribution — and higher EMS buying-power schedules stay
**inactive** unless the treasury clears the required solvency threshold (300% at
Phase 2, 500% at Phase 3). If a threshold isn't maintained, the system automatically
reverts to prior-phase rules. No participant holds a vested or guaranteed right to a
future buying-power rate before a threshold is cleared.

## Who holds what — the three-box structure

This separation is the legal and economic firewall that keeps charitable, community,
and founder interests apart. It is not cosmetic.

| Entity | Role | Key constraint |
|---|---|---|
| **Harmony for Hope, Inc. (H4H)** — 501(c)(3) | Charitable anchor, program sponsor and legal operator; fiduciary steward of the treasury | Cannot be a path to founder equity; operates under IRS nonprofit rules; **is not the money transmitter** |
| **MountainShares Commons / DUNA** — Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association | Community-owned ledger, EMS/M$ system, and DAO governance; the **money-services licensee of record** (WV Money Transmitter License, FinCEN MSB registration, surety bond, BSA/AML program, cash-out rails) | Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or profit-participation rights |
| **Kidd's Technical Services (KTS)** — to-be-formed WV LLC | Founder commercial engine; holder of the Ms. Allis / ALLIS platform IP | Not yet formed and has no EIN; founder-side rights do not activate until formation; entirely outside H4H charitable assets and community EMS |

No EMS hour, M$ balance, or community surplus allocation is a founder-equity path.
Founder-side value is documented separately, on the KTS side, under counsel-reviewed
terms.

## What it is not

- **Not a bank, credit union, investment advisor, or money transmitter.** H4H operates
  a closed-loop community rewards and prepaid-value program; the licensed money-services
  function sits with the DUNA.
- **Not making a securities claim either way.** The securities-law treatment of EMS, M$,
  and the phase-based conversion mechanism is **under review by securities counsel**, and
  this program makes no representation as to whether any of them is or is not a security.
- **Not a profit-extraction arrangement.** Returns are designed to follow people, place,
  and participation — not speculation or remote ownership.
